* What did you learn from implementing a multi-agent workflow?
While implementing this multi-agent travel planning system, it taught me the importance of role specialization such as a planner or reviewer in agent workflows. I think that clear task boundaries also will make each agent more effective. For example, the Planner would focus on structured itinerary creation and Reviewer would validate the feasibility using internet_search. Moreover, separating planning and validation will reduce bias and improved accuracy through iterative refinement. The Reviewer's delta list also creates specific and actionable feedback loop. Lastly, I learned that precise prompts and constraints will define agent success. For example, giving Planner clear structure guides and strict fact-checking guidelines in Reviewer to minimize hallucinations and ensure realistic and trustworthy travel plan.

* Challenges faced and how you addressed them.
One of the challenges I faced is how to best balance specificity, meaning that to what extent should I provide detailed structure for the Planner to ensure high quality without making outputs rigid. Therefore, I combined required formats with some flexible best practices to maintain creativity and consistency. Another challenge I faced is how to streamline the handoff between Planner and Reviewer. At the end, I optimized the Planner's output with clear formatting, and cost details to enable Reviewer to more efficiently verify information.
